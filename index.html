About

From Kubernetes clusters to XGBoost pipelines

I started my career keeping production Kubernetes clusters healthy at Atos-Syntel, working incident response, TLS renewals, and node-pool scaling for critical services. That hands-on infrastructure background is what I now bring into machine learning engineering: building models is one thing, but building them so they survive contact with real, messy, production data is another.

During my Master of Artificial Intelligence at RMIT, I interned at Prodegee, where I built a geospatial classification pipeline inside an agentic, version-controlled workflow — GitHub kanban tracking, peer review, and Kestra-orchestrated pipeline steps — that fed reproducible outputs to downstream teams.

I'm looking for AI/ML engineering roles where I can combine that infrastructure discipline with applied machine learning: feature engineering, model evaluation, and getting models into a state where other teams can actually rely on them.
